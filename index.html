The scaling thesis

Trade a vanity conversion rate for real volume

A high conversion rate on branded traffic is demand-harvesting. The growth story is demand-creation: paid ads + a site that converts cold traffic + a product for the mass market.

"I'd rather have a 3.5% conversion rate on 10x the traffic than 11% on today's."
Today's business is healthy - the 11% is a real achievement. The opportunity is to keep it AND add a flood of new, non-brand demand on top.

The proof: that 11% is a legacy-brand harvest

Here's the honest, encouraging read: the conversion rate is this high because almost everyone who arrives is already searching for you by name. Decades of reputation, great articles, and old YouTube videos still send warm, ready-to-buy visitors every day. That legacy is a massive asset most brands would kill for. It also means the 11% mostly reflects who is already looking - not how many strangers the site converts. That's exactly why "you convert at 11%" and "you're barely advertising or ranking for the category" are both true at the same time.

Modern e-commerce runs on Shopify: faster, always up, better for SEO, and it feeds clean product data to the AI shopping tools people increasingly buy through. It integrates with everything and pays for itself in conversion and uptime. Running serious e-commerce on legacy WordPress is a liability, not a saving.

Many of your customers are already in or near the trade, so opening that third door - with the "start your own business" videos and a dealer/wholesale funnel - lets you sell far more than one-off kits.

The hard part is behind you

Your trusted 20-year brand, proven product, dealer network, and operational infrastructure are already in place. The heavy lifting and investment are made. What's left is optimization - the highest-return work there is.

Build the flywheel

A modern funnel that acquires new customers from the category, converts them, upsells them, then keeps spinning - plus re-engaging the huge base of past customers with email/SMS, subscriptions, and new products like ceramic coating.

Reach new-car owners

New-vehicle owners are the perfect audience: a chip is inevitable, and lease-holders especially want to avoid wear-and-tear fees. We can target recent buyers and put a kit in their hands before they even need it.

On reaching new-car owners: raw DMV data is off-limits (the DPPA privacy law), but DPPA-compliant automotive data providers - S&P Global Mobility (Polk), Experian Automotive, and conquest/in-market list firms like Hedges & Company - sell targeted recent-buyer and in-market audiences sourced from finance, insurance, dealer and OEM data. So "mail a kit to people who just bought a car" is a real, buyable channel worth testing.
