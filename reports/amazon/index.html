The game plan

Five moves, in order.

1

Fix the listings + deploy A+ Content

Cheapest, fastest, highest-certainty win - and it's free since Brand Registry is done. Clean white-background hero images, infographic secondary images, benefit-driven bullets, and A+ modules (a comparison chart vs. cheap pens, the easy 3-step system, the brand story). This directly fixes "the ad looks bad, the competitor's looks good."

Amazon-published lift: +8% standard A+, up to +20% Premium
2

Turn the Brand Store into a real storefront

Rebuild the Store into a multi-page, vehicle-segmented experience (shop by make/model, by damage type, by kit) like ERA Paints - then switch on Brand Analytics to see exactly which search terms competitors are winning.

Store visitors: +53.9% purchase frequency, +71.3% AOV (Amazon)
3

Get the Prime badge - FBA (or Seller-Fulfilled Prime)

The Prime badge lifts conversion and Buy-Box share. Best path for a big long-tail catalog: a hybrid - FBA your top sellers, keep the slow per-vehicle SKUs FBM/SFP to avoid storage surcharges. SFP keeps fulfillment in-house but has strict 2025 requirements we'd confirm you can meet.

Prime badge ~+20-30% conversion (industry estimate)
4

Defensive + offensive Sponsored ads

Defend: run Sponsored Products/Display on your own ASINs and brand terms so competitors can't buy the "related products" strip and branded search on your pages. Attack: conquest competitor listings - especially ones priced higher or rated under 4 stars - so your ad shows on their pages.

Auto-category CPC ~$2.20; platform ACoS ~29.6%; ROAS 2.5-5x
5

Build reviews with Amazon Vine

Any hero SKU under 30 reviews goes into Vine to seed credible, verified reviews - which lift conversion and ad efficiency. Vine requires FBA, which reinforces move #3. New brands get a $200 Vine credit.

Vine: free for ≤2 units, $75 (3-10), $200 (11-30) per ASIN
