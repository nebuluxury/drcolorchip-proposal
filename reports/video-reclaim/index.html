Report 03 · YouTube & Google Ads

Reclaim your 20 years of video.

You spent two decades making the how-to and training videos that made you the experts - and they're all over YouTube with tens of thousands of views each. Right now competitors are buying ad space on those very videos and skimming off your audience. Here's exactly how we take it back.

The short answer

Yes - this can be done.

All four moves are standard, policy-compliant Google Ads practice: defend your own videos, retarget everyone who ever watched you, conquest competitors' videos, and lock down your branded search. The one thing you can't do is force a competitor's ad off a video - so instead of blocking them, we simply win the same ad slot on your content, where your relevance is impossible to beat.
Why it's happening

How a competitor lands on your video

YouTube sells the pre-roll/overlay on a video through the same live auction as Google Search. An advertiser can aim at your content three ways:

  • Managed placements - the sharp one. They paste your channel or specific video URLs and target them by name. This is how they show up specifically on your videos.
  • Keyword / topic / contextual - they target the subject ("touch up paint," topic "Automotive") and Google places them on matching videos, yours included.
  • Audience - they target the viewer (in-market "auto parts"), so they follow your audience around regardless of the video.
The key nuance: the only person who can turn ads off a video is the channel owner, and only if the channel is in the YouTube Partner Program. Even then you can only choose whether ads run - never which advertiser wins. There is no button anywhere that blocks another advertiser from placing ads on content. The correct response is to out-bid and out-relevance them on the same auction - which, on your own videos, you will almost always win.
The plan

Four moves to take it back.

1. Defend your own videos

Own-video placements

Run a video campaign whose placements are your channel + your top videos. Your ad plays as the pre-roll on your own content, so viewers see you first - not a competitor. Your relevance/Quality Score on your own videos is nearly impossible to outbid.

Branded-search defense

A cheap Search campaign on "dr colorchip" (and misspellings) so no competitor sits above you on your own name. Brand terms are low-cost and convert high; we watch Auction Insights weekly since Google won't warn you.

2. Reclaim & retarget the audience

YouTube viewer remarketing

Link the channel to Google Ads and build audiences of everyone who watched any video, watched specific videos, subscribed, or liked. Twenty years of views (organic and ad views count) becomes a retargeting pool most competitors could never build.

Demand Gen for purchases

Google's conversion-focused video campaign (the successor to Video Action / TrueView-for-action) drives "buy the kit" across YouTube, Discover & Gmail - fed by those remarketing audiences to turn views into orders.

3. Conquest the competitors

Competitor-video placements

Same placement mechanic, aimed at competitor channels & videos - your ad becomes the pre-roll on their content, catching their high-intent viewers at the exact moment they're shopping.

Competitor brand keywords (Search)

Bidding on a rival's name as a keyword is allowed (and upheld in U.S. courts). We keep their trademark out of the ad copy to stay policy-safe - and we police your mark if they use it in theirs.

4. Grow beyond the brand

Category search capture

Bid on high-intent, non-brand terms - "touch up paint," "car scratch repair kit," "auto paint chip repair." Bigger volume, earlier in the buying journey, and re-engaged later with the remarketing pool.

Format mix by goal

Skippable in-stream (pay-per-view) for efficient retargeting; 6-second bumpers (CPM) for cheap high-frequency reminders to past viewers; non-skippable only when the full message must land.

Suggested starting budget: $10-50/day is a normal test range. Start small on the two cheapest, highest-return moves (branded-search defense + own-video placements), measure your real CPV/CPM/CPA, then scale into remarketing, Demand Gen, and competitor conquesting.
How we'd roll it out

Build order

  • Phase 1 (cheap, immediate): branded-search defense + place your own ad on your own top videos.
  • Phase 2: link the channel, build the 20-year viewer-remarketing pool, and launch Demand Gen to convert them to purchases.
  • Phase 3: competitor-video placements + non-brand category search, once we have baseline performance data.
Worth knowing

Honest caveats

  • You can't evict competitors from your videos - only out-bid/out-relevance them. Budget it as an ongoing cost, not a one-time fix.
  • Retargeting audiences need ~100 active users (30 days) before they serve - your video library clears that instantly, but brand-new narrow segments may sit idle briefly.
  • Cost figures are directional benchmarks, not guarantees - we validate with a small live test, then scale on real numbers.
